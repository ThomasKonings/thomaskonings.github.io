About me

My name is Thomas Konings, I am a PhD student in astrophysics at the Institute of Astronomy (KU Leuven), in the team of Prof. Dr. Leen Decin. I specialise in modeling star-planet interactions and how they affect the atmospheres of exoplanets. Specifically, this means that I

  • write code to model atmospheric physics,
  • perform data analysis on synthetic and real data,
  • keep myself updated on scientific literature,
  • publish in peer-reviewed journals,
  • and take deep dives into anything that could advance my research.
I am enthusiastic about teaching and science communication. You'll regularly find me at outreach events, spreading the word about exoplanets and more.

Beyond my research

As much as I enjoy working in astronomy, I thoroughly like to broaden my horizon and expand my knowledge on different topics. My interests reach far beyond (astro)physics and I consider this an added value in my professional career. You can find an overview on this page of some of the additional trainings I followed throught the years.

Data science & AI

I am passionate about data science and artificial intelligence, and I am constantly seeking out new opportunities to learn and grow in these areas. I am fascinated by the power of data to drive insights and inform decision-making, and I believe that AI and machine learning have the potential to revolutionize a wide range of industries and applications. I am learning more and more about these topics through ongoing education and practical experience.

Business, innovation & entrepreneurship

While I do not have any formal education or professional experience in the world of business development, innovation, and entrepreneurship, I am in the processes of educating myself in these areas. I participate in courses, challenges and workshops to further learn and experience real-world scenarios.

Personal finance

I strongly belief in taking control of your financial situation in order to secure a healthy financial future. I am embedded in the Belgian financial independence scene and like to educate myself on topics such as stock market investing and financial planning, with a particular focus on passive index investing through ETFs. I am no way a financial advisor but I like to discuss the topic with anyone who is willing to.

Weather & Climatology

I am interested in the field of weather and climatology, particularly as it relates to climate change. As a member of a generation facing the crisis of climate change, I am curious about understanding the factors contributing to this issue and finding solutions to mitigate its impact. Through my PhD studies and additional education, I like to deepen my knowledge of weather patterns and climate systems, as well as the current efforts to address climate change.

Personal growth & self-development

I am committed to cultivating a growth mindset and continually pushing myself to learn and improve in all areas of my life. I believe that investing in my personal development is essential for achieving long-term success and fulfillment. Through coaching and self-reflection, I am able to gain valuable insights into my strengths, weaknesses, and areas for improvement, and I use this knowledge to fuel my personal growth and achieve my goals.

Professional sports career

Having competed at the highest national level and in international competitions with both clubs and the national team until age 22, I was forced to end my volleyball career due to a severe shoulder injury. Although no longer active at the highest level, the valuable lessons I learned as a professional athlete continue to shape my professional career and personal life. The discipline, and resilience I developed after spending my youth and adolescence in a high performing and competitive environment has helped me to navigate challenging situations in my professional endeavors. Furthermore, my experience balancing a daily training schedule with academic pursuits has taught me the importance of time management and the value of hard work in achieving success.
