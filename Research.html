PhD Research & Publications

PhD research

I specialize in numerical modeling of exoplanet atmospheres with a focus on analyzing how host star interactions impact their physical and chemical state. In my previous research, I have explored the effects of stellar flares on close-orbiting gas giants and how they affect spectral features in transmission spectra.

Currently, I am involved in the JWST GTO MIRI program and am modeling a planet observed by the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST). Additionally, I am investigating the survivability of exoplanetary atmospheres during the asymptotic giant branch (AGB) phase of their host star.
